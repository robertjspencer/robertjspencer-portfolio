Professional Summary

I am a Data Scientist and incoming PhD Researcher who builds end-to-end AI systems to translate complex data into measurable, real-world outcomes.

Commencing in April 2026, I will be undertaking a PhD in Artificial Intelligence at The University of Queensland, supported by a fully funded RTP scholarship and a SAAFE CRC top-up. My research will focus on developing Cross-Sectoral Digital Twins for Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) risk assessment.

Before returning to academia full-time, I was the Lead Data Scientist at Radix Nutrition. There, I led the design and deployment of proprietary machine-learning pipelines to optimise product formulations and substantiate data-driven health claims. My work sits comfortably at the intersection of data science and software engineering; I enjoy not just researching models, but building robust infrastructure that actually ships.

Research Interests

  • Digital Twins — Cross-sectoral modelling of complex systems for risk assessment and forecasting
  • Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) — AI-driven approaches to understanding and mitigating AMR risks
  • Transfer Learning — Leveraging pre-trained models for time-series forecasting with limited data
  • Transformer Architectures — Comparative studies and novel applications for energy and health domains
  • Synthetic Data Generation — Creating realistic datasets for training and validation

Career Journey

My path to AI research has been anything but linear — and that's exactly what makes it valuable. I've worked across multiple sectors, from health promotion and community health to elite sports and nutrition technology.

Health & Community Background

Before transitioning to data science, I worked in health promotion and community health services. At St John New Zealand and the Waikato District Health Board, I developed a deep appreciation for how data can drive public health outcomes.

Sports & Performance

My passion for strength and conditioning stemmed from a background in Olympic weightlifting and an undergraduate degree in Human Performance Science. At the Waikato Rowing Club, I applied evidence-based training methodologies to help athletes achieve their potential.

Academic Background

I hold a Master's in Analytics (Health) from Massey University and am concurrently completing a Master's in Computer Science (AI) at Monash University. My previous academic research on transfer learning and Transformer models advanced building-energy forecasting and culminated in a first-author Q1 publication.
